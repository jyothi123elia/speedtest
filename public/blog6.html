How to Fix Slow Internet — Complete Guide 2025

Slow internet is a common problem that affects millions of people in India every day. Whether you are using Jio, Airtel, BSNL or any other provider, these steps will help you diagnose and fix slow internet problems quickly.

Step 1 — Test Your Current Speed

Before trying any fix, test your current internet speed. This gives you a baseline to compare after making changes. Use our free speed test tool for instant results.

Step 2 — Restart Everything

Turn off your router and modem completely. Wait 60 seconds. Turn them back on and wait 2 minutes for full restart. Test your speed again — this fixes 40% of slow internet problems instantly.

Step 3 — Check for Background Apps

Many apps use internet in the background without you knowing. On Windows open Task Manager and check Network column. On phone go to Settings → Data Usage to see which apps use most data.

Step 4 — Clear DNS Cache

On Windows open Command Prompt as administrator and type: ipconfig /flushdns — this clears old DNS data that can slow down browsing speed significantly.

Step 5 — Change DNS Server

Your ISP's default DNS servers are often slow. Switch to Google DNS for faster browsing. Set your DNS to 8.8.8.8 and 8.8.4.4 in your network settings.

Step 6 — Check for Viruses

Malware and viruses can use your internet bandwidth secretly. Run a full system scan with Windows Defender or any trusted antivirus software.

Step 7 — Update Network Drivers

Outdated network drivers can cause slow speeds on Windows computers. Go to Device Manager → Network Adapters → right click → Update Driver.

Step 8 — Contact Your ISP

If none of these steps work, contact your internet service provider. Show them your speed test results as proof. You may be entitled to compensation or a free technician visit if speeds are consistently below your plan.

When to Switch Internet Provider

If your ISP consistently delivers less than 60% of your promised speed even after multiple complaints, it is time to switch to a better provider. Compare plans and speeds in your area before switching.
